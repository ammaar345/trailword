How to Play TrailWord

A daily five-letter word puzzle with a category and a clue.

The goal

Every day, TrailWord picks one secret five-letter word. Your job is to guess it in six tries or fewer. Unlike a plain word-guessing game, you are not starting blind: each puzzle shows you a category (for example, Nature, Food, or Science) and a short clue that nudges you toward the answer. Use both to make your opening guess count.

Making a guess

Type any real five-letter English word and press Enter. TrailWord checks your guess against a dictionary of nearly sixteen thousand valid words, so obscure-but-real words are accepted while random letter strings are rejected with a gentle shake. After you submit, every tile changes color to tell you how close you are.

Reading the colors

Right letter, right spot. This letter belongs here — lock it in.
Right letter, wrong spot. The word contains this letter, but somewhere else.
Not in the word. Skip this letter for the rest of the puzzle.

The on-screen keyboard updates with the same colors, so you always have a running record of which letters are still in play. A colorblind-friendly palette with shape patterns is available in Settings.

Hints when you are stuck

Tap the Hint button for a nudge that never solves the puzzle for you. The first hint tells you a letter the word contains; the second reveals a different letter; a further hint can point to one exact position. Hints are limited per puzzle so the challenge stays intact.

Daily, Practice, and Custom trails

The Daily puzzle is the same word for everyone and resets at midnight UTC — a countdown shows how long until the next one. Practice mode gives you unlimited puzzles with no guess limit, perfect for warming up. Custom trails let you enter any five-letter word to build a puzzle to share with a friend.

Streaks and stats

Solve the Daily puzzle on consecutive days to build a streak. TrailWord keeps your win percentage, current and best streaks, and a distribution of how many guesses you usually need. All of it is stored privately in your browser — there are no accounts and nothing is uploaded.
